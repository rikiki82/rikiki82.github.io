Vegetable purée

For 4 persons (~1 hour)
  • 4 carrots
  • 4 potatoes
  • 1 parnsnip
  • 1 leek
  • 1 or 2 turnips
  • 1 sweet potato
  • 2 vegetable stock cubes
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons of crème fraîche
  • salt
  • black pepper
  1. Peel the vegetables and cut them in big cubes. Warning! For the leek:
    Cut the top of the dark leaves, cut in half from 6 cm of the bottom to the top, do it again so it's cut in 4 but make sure to keep the base so it's still in 1 piece. Rince well between the leaves.
  2. Put all the vegetables in a pan with water and cook for 30 min.
  3. Remove the water and mix the vegetables with the stock cubes and the crème fraîche.
  4. Add salt and pepper to you liking and it's ready!

Caramel pork

For 2 persons (~45 minutes)
  • 300 to 400g of pork tenderloin
  • 400g of sugar
  • nuoc mam sauce
  • soy sauce
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 onion
  • ginger
  1. Mince the onion and garlic.
  2. Brown them over low heat until he sweats (when it become transparent or golden) and then keep them in a bowl.
  3. In an other frying pan, pour the sugar and heat over medium heat. If it's too hot, the sugar will burn and you won't have the caramel flavour. Watch closely, it shouldn't smell like burnt and do not stir much. It should take 10 to 15 minutes.
  4. When the caramel is ready (the sugar is molten and brown), add the pork cut in 5cm cubes with the onion and garlic.
  5. Add 2 tablespoons of nuoc mam sauce and 2 of soy sauce. The liquid should almost cover the meat so add water accordingly.
  6. Cook for 20 minutes at medium heat and add more water if it evaporates too much.
  7. You just have to prepare the side, it can go with everything eventhough I think it's better with rice.

Maple syrup chicken

For 2 persons (20 minutes)
  • 300 to 400g of chicken
  • maple syrup
  • 1 lemon
  • soy sauce
  • curry
  • 2 onions
  1. Dice the chicken.
  2. Mince the onions and brown them. Make sure to add some oil or butter in the frying pan beforehand.
  3. When it's starting to brown, add at least 300ml of maple syrup (~400g), some lemon juice, 8 tablespoons of soy sauce , some curry and some water.
  4. Now you have to wait until you see the meat is cooked. In the meantime, taste the sauce, you can add maple syrup if it's not too runny, lemon juice if it's too sweet or curry if it's too bland.
  5. For the side you can do what you want but I strongly recommend semolina because it absorbs really well the sauce and it tastes really good.

Carbonara pasta

For 1 person (15 minutes)
  • 150 of pasta
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons of crème fraîche
  • 50g of (smoked) bacon lardons
  1. Cook your pasta (follow the instructions on the packaging).
  2. It depends on your hotplate but I personally start cooking my lardons 7 minutes before pasta is ready at medium heat.
  3. When the lardons are grilled, deglaze with the crème fraîche.
  4. Add the lardons and sauce to the pasta and stir well. When you are ready to eat, add the yolk and enjoy!
